About Kerala

Kerala, often referred to as "God's Own Country," is a state located in the southern part of India. Known for its lush greenery, serene backwaters, and pristine beaches, Kerala offers a unique blend of nature, culture, and heritage.

Top Places to Visit

  • Alleppey Backwaters: Experience the tranquility of houseboats and lagoons.
  • Munnar: A hill station known for its tea plantations and scenic views.
  • Kochi: Explore the historic Fort Kochi and Chinese fishing nets.
  • Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary: Perfect for wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers.

Culture of Kerala

Kerala's culture is deeply rooted in traditions, festivals, and art forms. Kathakali, a classical dance form, and Onam, the state's harvest festival, are iconic representations of its rich heritage.

Delicious Kerala Cuisine

Kerala's cuisine is a flavorful blend of spices and coconut. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Appam and Stew
  • Karimeen Pollichathu (Pearl Spot Fish)
  • Puttu and Kadala Curry
  • Banana Chips
